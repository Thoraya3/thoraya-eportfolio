# Launch into Computing – Unit 5 Reflection
## Data Science and Storage

---

## What? (Description)

In Unit 5, I studied the fundamentals of Data Science and the role of data storage technologies in managing and analysing large volumes of data within enterprise systems. The unit covered key concepts such as structured and unstructured data, relational and non‑relational databases, Big Data characteristics, and the fundamentals of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML). The Lecturecast also explored real‑world applications of Data Science and AI across industries, as well as ethical and legal considerations such as data privacy, bias, and GDPR compliance.

As part of this unit, I completed the required reading on Data Science, Big Data technologies, and AI applications. I also participated in the Case Study forum on Netflix, where I analysed how Data Science techniques and NoSQL storage support large‑scale recommendation systems while raising privacy and ethical challenges. In addition, I completed the Data Analytics with Python and Data Storage Reflection assignment, which involved loading and cleaning a dataset, performing basic statistical analysis, creating visualisations, and reflecting on the suitability of different storage models.

---

## So what? (Interpretation)

This unit highlighted the importance of aligning data analytics techniques with appropriate storage architectures. Data Science is only effective when data can be stored, accessed, and processed efficiently. Comparing SQL and NoSQL storage models reinforced how structured analytical datasets benefit from relational databases, while high‑volume, fast‑changing, or unstructured data often requires scalable NoSQL or cloud‑based solutions.

From a professional perspective, these concepts strongly relate to my work in telecom and enterprise environments, where systems generate large volumes of transactional and event‑based data. Understanding when to use SQL, NoSQL, or hybrid storage approaches helps ensure scalability, performance, and governance. The emphasis on ethics, GDPR, and bias also reinforced the responsibility of managing data securely and transparently, particularly when analytics and AI influence automated decision‑making.

---

## What next? (Action)

Going forward, I will apply Data Science principles more deliberately by considering data quality, storage design, and ethical implications at the early stages of solution design. I plan to strengthen my practical skills in Python‑based analytics and continue evaluating storage technologies based on enterprise requirements rather than convenience.

Academically, I will continue documenting analytics activities and reflections in my e‑portfolio to link theory with practice. The knowledge gained in this unit provides a strong foundation for future modules involving AI systems, data‑driven decision‑making, and large‑scale enterprise analytics.
